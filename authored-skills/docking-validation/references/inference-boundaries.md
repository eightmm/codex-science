# Docking inference boundaries

Read before writing pose, ranking, affinity, selectivity, mechanism, or translational claims.

## Distinct outputs

Keep separate:

- generated pose;
- pose confidence;
- docking or rescoring value;
- ranking performance;
- predicted affinity;
- calibrated binding probability;
- experimental affinity;
- cellular or in vivo effect.

A favorable docking score is not experimental affinity. A plausible pose is not a mechanism. A benchmark average is not evidence for one target–ligand pair outside the benchmark domain.

## Required external evidence

Affinity claims require assay-aware evidence with units, relation qualifiers, endpoint, conditions, and uncertainty. Mechanism claims require orthogonal structural, biochemical, mutational, kinetic, or cellular evidence as appropriate.

## Applicability

Report target family, receptor states, ligand classes, microstates, covalent or metal chemistry, size and flexibility range, and training-overlap state. Keep unsupported cases exploratory or abstain.

## Claim downgrade

When essential support is absent, downgrade from mechanistic to pose hypothesis, from affinity to ranking hypothesis, from generalization to within-benchmark performance, or from supported to inconclusive.
